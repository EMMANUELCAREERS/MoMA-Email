The artworks on view in our galleries are an endless source of inspiration. This week we’re inviting you to dive into stories, personal reflections, and interviews that illuminate these works beyond our walls.
Writer and professor Adam F. Bradley celebrates Ralph Ellison and the ways he has inspired artists like Jeff Wall, whose vivid photograph (on view in Gallery 409: Underground) brings to life Invisible Man’s underground lair and the 1,369 light bulbs covering the ceiling. A MoMA staff member shares his excitement in finding the familiar in the many layers of Alfonso Ossorio’s work (on view in Gallery 415: Divided States of America). And artists Carolina Caycedo and David de Rozas (featured in the newest Projects exhibition join Carrizo/Comecrudo Tribe of Texas chairman Juan Mancias for a conversation on how the past can inform the fight for environmental justice today.
Make your own connections with these works and more on Magazine, and, if you haven’t already, make plans to visit them in person.
